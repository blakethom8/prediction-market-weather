## Root Cause Analysis

### Problem 1: Edge Strategy is Structurally Broken

Buying YES at 1-6¢ on temperature buckets requires sub-1°F accuracy. We don't have that. The two losses:
- `KXHIGHLAX-26APR13-B62.5` YES @ 3¢ — actual was 66.5°F (4.5°F miss). Expected edge 0.62 was fake.
- `KXHIGHMIA-26APR13-B79.5` YES @ 19¢ — actual was 81.5°F (1.5°F miss).

The "edge" was from comparing our model's point estimate against market price. But our point estimate has ±3-5°F uncertainty. Market prices at 64-66¢ for the 81-82°F Miami bucket were the *correct* answer. We were fighting informed traders with bad data.

**Diagnosis from Claude Code analysis**: `choose_best_market()` converts a single NWS forecast into a bucket probability with no bias correction, no ensemble uncertainty, and no volume filter. A point estimate with unknown uncertainty isn't a probability — it's a guess in math clothing.

### Problem 2: Systematic Cool Bias

Every settled edge bet ran warmer than our forecast. Observed pattern:
- LA: predicted 62°F, actual 66.5°F (+4.5°F)
- Miami: predicted 80°F, actual 81.5°F (+1.5°F)
- DC (Mar 25): market was right, we were wrong (1.5°F miss)
- Seattle (Mar 25): market was right, we were wrong (2.5°F miss)

The +1.5-4.5°F systematic warm bias means our YES bets on lower buckets are almost always wrong. We're buying the wrong side of the distribution.

**Root cause**: NWS forecasts use metro-area coordinates, not airport-specific station coordinates. Airports run warmer (urban heat island, river valleys). This is documented in BETTING_INSIGHTS.md but NOT applied in the code.

### Problem 3: Volume-Blind Recommendations

The scan treats a 500-contract market the same as a 25,000-contract market. High-volume markets have informed traders who've already corrected for station bias. If the market disagrees with us at 7,000+ contracts, the market is almost certainly right.

## Improvement Recommendations (Claude Code Analysis)

### Fix 1: Apply Bias Correction (Highest Priority)

Add `+1.5°F` additive correction to raw NWS forecast before any probability calculation:

```python
WARM_BIAS_CORRECTION_F = 1.5  # systematic cool bias observed over 10+ settled bets
if forecast_high_f is not None:
    forecast_high_f += WARM_BIAS_CORRECTION_F
```

Location: `src/weatherlab/forecast/asos.py` where `forecast_high_f` is assembled.

This alone would have flipped several losses into non-bets.

### Fix 2: Volume Filter

Skip or flag contracts with <1,000 total contracts. If volume >5K AND market disagrees with model by >10¢, output `MARKET_DISAGREES` rather than BUY.

### Fix 3: Use Observed Temps (Already Collected, Not Used)
